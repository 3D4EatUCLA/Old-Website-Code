3D4E at UCLA

DESIGN.TINKER.PRINT


3D4E is an intercollegiate network of 3D printing and rapid-prototyping enthusiasts.

Chapters span across the southwest, including UCLA, University of Southern California, San Diego State University, University of Arizona, and many others, with membership constantly expanding. Our club’s main focus is on group projects, in which we design, manufacture, and test using CAD software and 3D printing. These projects range from recreational, like RC cars, to life-changing, like our prosthetic hand. In addition to our group efforts, 3D4E also supports members with the resources and expertise they might need to pursue their own individual ideas.

3D4E at UCLA is generously sponsored by AIO Robotics, New Matter, Robo3D, and XYZ. Their donations of printers and filament make our club's endeavours possible. Space for storage and operation of our printers is furnished by the UCLA Library system. We are also sponsored by Blackstone LaunchPad, a start-up space that supports club events.

Musical Instruments

If you combine 3D printing, a love for music, a desire to help people, and a little bit of creativity, this is the project that results. We are designing and printing multiple different instruments with the intention of donating these to causes in which they can make the largest positive impact, like homeless shelters and orphanages. The instruments will include various ukuleles, drums, recorders, and whatever else our imagination leads to.

Prosthetic "Spock" Hand

Inspired by e-Nabling the Future’s Raptor Reloaded prosthetic, the tendon-driven Spock is a 3D printed prosthetic hand that enables users missing a hand to grasp, dribble, and shoot a basketball. While the base design of the hand depends on wrist motion, the Spock can also be modified to assist those without a wrist. Deemed “The Spock” for its similar appearance to the signature Vulcan greeting gesture, this prosthetic hand allowed our young recipient to make 17 hoops within an hour of practice.

Chess Set

Members who are new to CAD can get up to speed by designing their own chess pieces. Pieces are as simple or complex as desired, and project participants are free to design pieces in the standard style or be as creative as they like. In addition to the individual designs, members can attend short lessons where we tackle one Fusion 360 feature at a time.

Renewable Energy

3D printing potentially plays a large role in renewable energy structures. During fall quarter of the 2016-17 year, 3D4E will be taking on the task of developing a modular wind turbine iPhone charger, engaging individuals interested in electronics, design, and aerospace. The idea of the turbine is to allow one to assemble and disassemble it freely while generating electricity without the need of an outlet. In the winter, the team will dive into the creation of larger renewable energy devices, particularly looking into vertical axis wind turbines with a possible implementation of solar panels.
