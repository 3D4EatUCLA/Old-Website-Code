3D4E is an intercollegiate network of 3D printing and rapid-prototyping enthusiasts.

Chapters span across the southwest, including UCLA, University of Southern California, San Diego State University, University of Arizona, and many others, with membership constantly expanding. Our club’s main focus is on group projects, in which we design, manufacture, and test using CAD software and 3D printing. These projects range from recreational, like RC cars, to life-changing, like our prosthetic hand. In addition to our group efforts, 3D4E also supports members with the resources and expertise they might need to pursue their own individual ideas.

3D4E at UCLA is sponsored by AIO Robotics, a Los Angeles-based printer company that generously provides us with the printers and filament necessary to pursue our hands-on design projects. Space for storage and operation of our printers is furnished by the UCLA Library system. We are also sponsored by Blackstone LaunchPad, a start-up space that supports club events.

Prosthetic "Spock" Hand

Inspired by e-Nabling the Future’s Raptor Reloaded prosthetic, the tendon-driven Spock is a 3D printed prosthetic hand that enables users missing a hand to grasp, dribble, and shoot a basketball. While the base design of the hand depends on wrist motion, the Spock can also be modified to assist those without a wrist. Deemed “The Spock” for its similar appearance to the signature Vulcan greeting gesture, this prosthetic hand allowed our young recipient to make 17 hoops within an hour of practice.
